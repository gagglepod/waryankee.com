## Greetings!

I am Kyle M. Bondo -- US Navy veteran, amateur historian, and professional podcaster -- and I would like the thank you for coming with me on this journey!

I have been following the history of the American Civil War since I was a knee-high to a grasshopper. After endlessly exploring almost every corner of every preserved battlefield in Northern Virginia, I created this podcast to share what I have learned with you.

I don't always get everything right and I often have to overcome some linguistic challenges (e.g. saying cavalry instead of calvary), but my hope is to create a podcast that educates as much as it entertains.

My one goal is to create an American Civil War series that I can give to my Granddaughter so that she will one day learn what happened here and what it cost to carry out the promise of the [Declaration of Independence](https://www.archives.gov/founding-docs/declaration-transcript).

Thank you for taking the time to listen. You are appreciated!

## About the Show

War Yankee - Overland, is my American Civil War history podcast that follows General Ulysses S. Grant and the Army of the Potomac on its 47-day, 113-mile military campaign south from Culpeper to Petersburg, Virginia. I go over all the background of why I created War Yankee on the very first episode here:

- [Overland 01: Perspective](https://waryankee.com/wy-overland-01-perspective/)

## How to Listen

War Yankee has to seperate tracks for you to follow:

- [War Yankee - Overland](https://waryankee.com/category/overland/) is a stand alone series following Grant's Overland Campaign as it happened during May/June 1864. I pull all my information from several sources (both dusty and digital) and do my best to be as accurate as possible.

- [War Yankee - Intelligence](https://waryankee.com/category/intelligence/) is where I started putting all the research notes that didn't fit into the Overland series. These are one-off episodes that focus on a very specific topic.

## Podcast Production History

War Yankee - Overland was my 7th podcast and the first Civil War history podcast crated for Gagglepod. Premiering in May 2021, the show as an attempt to document my real world tour of every battlefield, skimish, and road-side marker that was connected to Grant's Overland Campaign from May to June 1864.

For 3-months, I spent every day retracing the 113-mile path of Grant's Army of the Potomac from their first step out of Culpeper, Virginia, to their last step at the onset of the Seige of Petersburg, Virginia. Each day I matched the historical event from 156 years before with the real location, explored area, researched the people involved, and tried to understand why each battle went the way it did.

The journey became very personal to me as I discovered countless heroic yet horrifying stories of war, and stood where hundreds (if not thousands) of Americans had been wounded or killed. I cannot tell you how many graveyards visited nor how many isolated and overgrown gravesites I found along the way. But what I can tell you is that you cannot comprehend how many soldiers died during this 47-day campaign until you walk the path. It is number that quickly becomes overwhelming when you see where it all happened yourself and truly understand why historians call Virginia the most _expensive real-estate in the United States of America_.

Meawnhile, while I did manage to complete my real world battlefield tour in July 2021, I was only capable of producing the first 12 episodes of the podcast before we had to close our podcast production studio Gagglepod, in October 2021. Over the next year, I tried to restart War Yankee several times but was unable to come back to it while I worked on other projects. It wasn't until I had the opportunity to visit the Vicksburg battlefield in Mississippi that _War Yankee_ called back to me.

Now, after over a year on hiatus, War Yankee - Overland will returned to podcasting in 2023. First, I moved the show to my own podcast media hosting company called [Oncetold](https://oncetold) in Fort Worth, Texas. Then I rebuilt the website at [waryankee.com](https://waryankee.com/) and wrote the script outlines for 20 new episodes (covering the entire Battle of the Wilderness). All that is left to do is start recording. Wish me luck!
